Solution:

Since non-organic waste does not decompose, our technology will be primarily for preserving organic waste.
If we were dealing with some other type of material, we could implement cooling systems, but organic waste
decomposes even at cool temperatures (fridge) unless we freeze it.

However, freezing would not be very cost effective because it requires power at all times.

We therefore opted to dry and heat the trash instead of freezing it and here is why:

  • Organic waste usually spoils because of the fungus and bacteria that feed on it.
  • These microbes usually thrive in humid and cool places
  • If we get rid of their environment, we can prevent organic waste from decomposing
  • In this case dehydration deals with the moisture and thermal sterilization with the low temperatures

With this in mind, we can introduce a trash processing units into the communities and urban areas where the trash
will be processed and stored until the storage tanks become full and sensors signal the collectors to pick it up.

Advantages

  • This solution enables both parties to leverage from it.

  • The trash can be disposed at an time

  • No massive amounts of trash in the house

  • Reduction of land pollution

  • There will be no awful stenches spreading around

  • The waste in the units can be compressed to prevent overflow

  • Waste collection companies will be able deploy their employees less frequently

  • The solution is cost-effective in the long run

Disadvantages

  • The trash processing units require highly complex technologies

  • Consumers will have to carry the trash to the nearest unit

  • Constructing the units is costly at the beginning of the project
